Kritisha Jain's UX Portfolio

Hi, I'm Kritisha Jain.

I'm a UX Researcher and Design Strategist with 8+ years of global experience in human-centered innovation. My work lives at the intersection of engineering tools, AI systems, and social justice — from shaping MATLAB workflows used by 20,000+ developers, to co-designing therapy chatbots for survivors of violence in India.

At MathWorks, I lead end-to-end research for core MATLAB products, advising 20+ teams across real-time systems, model-based design, and autonomous technologies. My research has influenced 40+ features and helped cut delivery timelines by 15%. I’ve conducted 50+ user studies and delivered insights that shifted roadmap priorities — all while making platforms more accessible and equitable.

A graduate of the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT), I bring a systems approach to UX — blending participatory research, storytelling, and strategy. My past work spans the MIT Legatum Center, Mastercard Foundation, and American Association of University Women, where I built platforms to empower low-income women with economic independence through tech and design.

Whether it’s orchestrating national protests or mentoring junior women in design, I lead with empathy, rigor, and a vision for impact. My work has been featured by British Vogue, MIT D-Lab, and the Womxn in STEM Symposium, and I’m just getting started.
